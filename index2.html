Supporting missions in Philippines

In November 2013, the typhoon Haiyan devastated most of the Philippines leaving more than 7,000 dead and about 11 million people affected according to the United Nations. It is considered one of the most violent typhoon ever recorded with winds varying between 230 and 315 km/h. Like many other expatriates, Jane Souterre has watched helplessly this natural disaster. Ugsod Banga, her home village where she still has several family members, has unfortunately been completely wiped out. Jane Souterre immediately mobilized to help the victims of this tragedy. Three days later, the association Coeurs à Coeurs was born with the aim to help victims rebuild their homes. In just a few days, and thanks to the generosity of donors, the association has allowed the construction of 44 shelters of 20m2, each of which could accommodate a family. Food, clothing and medicines were sent in the following weeks.

Today, Coeurs à Coeurs continues to support the worst-off, allowing access to hospital care, sending materials, clothing and food, assistance can manifest in many forms. More recently, the victims of typhoon Nona in December 2015 could count on Coeurs à Coeurs support.
